Daily Precautions to Keep Children Safe

Small, regular steps can prevent emergencies and help children feel more secure. These tips support, but do not replace, your doctor‚Äôs plan.

Blood Sugar and Safety Checks

  • Check blood sugar as often as your doctor suggests (with a glucometer or sensor).
  • Keep a ‚Äúhypo kit‚Äù ready: fast sugar (juice, glucose tablets, sweets) and a follow-up snack.
  • Teach your child signs of low sugar: shakiness, sweating, sudden sleepiness, confusion, or irritability.
  • Teach trusted adults (teachers, relatives, coaches) what to do in an emergency.
  • Have contact numbers for parents and doctors written and saved in phones.

Food and Meal Precautions

  • Try to keep meals and snacks at regular times when possible.
  • Include vegetables, protein, and healthy fats to avoid sudden sugar spikes.
  • Be careful with sugary drinks and sweets. Match with insulin or activity as advised by your doctor.
  • Pack safe snacks for school and outings, especially if plans change or meals are delayed.

Activity and School Precautions

  • Before sports or heavy play, check sugar and give extra snacks if the doctor suggests.
  • Explain to teachers and coaches that the child may need to stop and eat or check sugar.
  • Keep supplies at school: test strips, hypo treatment, snacks, and written instructions.
  • For school trips, ensure an adult knows the diabetes plan and emergency steps.

  • Never stop insulin suddenly without clear medical advice.
  • Seek urgent help if your child has trouble breathing, severe stomach pain, or is very sleepy.
These precautions are general and may not fit every child. Always follow the personalised plan from your healthcare team.
