What Causes Diabetes in Children?

Understanding why diabetes happens can reduce guilt and confusion. In most cases, it is not anyone‚Äôs fault. Diabetes is a medical condition, not a punishment or a result of something you did wrong.

Type 1 Diabetes

Most common in young children
  • The immune system mistakenly attacks the insulin-making cells in the pancreas.
  • Over time, the body makes little or no insulin, so blood sugar levels rise.
  • It is often linked to genetics and immune triggers, not to sugar intake or lifestyle.
  • Children with Type 1 diabetes need insulin therapy, but can still live full, active lives.

Parents do not ‚Äúcause‚Äù Type 1 diabetes by giving certain foods or sweets. It is a complex medical condition.

Type 2 Diabetes

Becoming more common in teens
  • The body still makes insulin, but the cells do not respond to it properly (insulin resistance).
  • It can be linked to family history, body weight, and low physical activity.
  • Healthy eating, regular movement, and medications or insulin may be used to manage it.

Other Types of Diabetes

  • Genetic forms: Certain rare genes can affect how the body makes or uses insulin.
  • Secondary diabetes: Some medicines or medical conditions can lead to diabetes.
  • Gestational history: Babies of mothers with gestational diabetes may have a higher risk later, but this does not guarantee diabetes.

Only a doctor or diabetes specialist can explain the exact type for your child and what it means for treatment.
